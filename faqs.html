Can I apply for a PWD ID for my child? expand_more

Yes, guardians/parents are highly encouraged to help their child with disability/disabilities to fill out the application for them.

How long is the PWD ID valid? expand_more

PWD IDs at Cagayan de Oro are valid for 3–5 years.

Is there any fee for PWD ID? expand_more

All PWD IDs are completely free of charge.

What should I do if I forgot my reference code? expand_more

First, check the confirmation message along with the reference code sent to your phone number. If you cannot find it, click the "Forgot Reference Code" option on the lookup window to trigger a new SMS text verification code. If neither option works, contact Cheryl directly at 0953-1122-974 for manual assistance.

What should I do if I lost my PWD ID? expand_more

Submit an Affidavit of Loss to the Persons with Disability Affairs Office (PDAO) - CDO to request a replacement replacement card.

Eligibility & Types of Disability

Who is eligible for a PWD ID? expand_more

Any Filipino citizen with a long-term physical, mental, intellectual, or sensory impairment that limits daily activities is eligible. The Department of Health (DOH) officially recognizes 7 categories:

• Psychosocial Disability: Severe Depression, Anxiety, Bipolar, or Schizophrenia.
• Orthopedic (Physical): Amputations, paralysis, or severe scoliosis.
• Visual: Severe visual impairment or total blindness that cannot be corrected by standard glasses.
• Hearing: Moderate to profound hearing loss in one or both ears.
• Speech and Language: Conditions affecting verbal communication and articulation.
• Intellectual: Down Syndrome, Global Developmental Delay, etc.
• Learning: Neurological processing conditions such as Autism, ADHD, or Dyslexia.

Are temporary disabilities eligible for a PWD ID? expand_more

No. Standard PWD IDs are issued exclusively to individuals with permanent or long-term conditions. Temporary medical injuries do not qualify.

Are non-apparent disabilities covered? expand_more

Yes. Non-apparent conditions (such as mental health conditions or psychosocial impairments) are covered, provided you can present an official medical certificate from an accredited clinical specialist (e.g., a psychiatrist).

Process & Timeline

How long does the online registration review take? expand_more

Applications are typically screened within 3 to 5 working days. However, due to high application volumes, verification can sometimes take 7 to 9 days. If your tracking status hasn't changed after 9 days, we highly recommend reaching out to PDAO for a manual review.

How will I know if my application is approved or rejected? expand_more

Click the REGISTRATION STATUS action button on the home page and input your unique Reference Code to view real-time tracking updates (Approved, Pending, or Rejected).

Other Concerns

Can someone else apply or claim my ID on my behalf? expand_more

Yes. Guardians and parents can complete the filing or claim the physical card with proof of guardianship. Any other representative must present a formal signed authorization letter from the applicant alongside their valid identification documents.

Once approved, where and how do I claim my physical ID card? expand_more

You can claim your physical ID card at the PDAO-CDO office, located within the Cagayan de Oro City Hall under the City Social Welfare Department (CSWD).

What if an establishment refuses to honor my PWD ID? expand_more

All business establishments are legally mandated to honor your privileges under RA 10754. You can report any violations directly to the National Council on Disability Affairs (NCDA) or to PDAO-CDO for legal enforcement.
